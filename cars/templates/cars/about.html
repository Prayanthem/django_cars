About the creator

My name is Petter Fredrik Hemnes, and I'm a CEMS MIM and MSc in Economics and Business Administration student at the Norwegian School of Economics (NHH).

About the project

I created crappycars as a programmatic response to a side-project my father (Anders) and my sister's boyfriend (Adrian) had ventured into in the spring of 2019. Adrian loves working with cars - he loves polishing them, modifying them, pimping them - and so my father had the idea that they could , using his capital, buy cars cheaply off of government auction websites, fix them up and resell them. Basically creating a small car flipping business. When I heard of this I wondered how they defined cheap, and found that I could use what I had learned in my study to improve on the evaluation.

It seemed expedient to me that they should calculate the maximum price they were willing to bid for a car by calculating (often called reservation price):

  • The cost of reparal
  • cost of capital (in this case cost of equity) - (the return they require to invest [synoynmous to the opportunity cost])
  • resale value

    • They would then invest if they could purchase a car for something equal to or lower than (reservation price =) resale value - cost of reparal - cost of capital

    What I could help with was creating a GUI which they could use to find resale value, and also cars which where under the reservation price. With this I created crappycars.
